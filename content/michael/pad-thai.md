## Ingredients

### Sauce

- 42g (1.5 oz) tamarind pulp + 120ml (½ cup) boiling water to dissolve *(or 90ml / 6 tbsp tamarind concentrate)*
- 36g (3 tbsp) dark brown sugar (or palm sugar)
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp Thai black soy sauce (Healthy Boy brand recommended)
- 1 tsp Thai sweet soy sauce (optional)
- ¼ tsp white pepper

### Stir-Fry

- 225g (8 oz) dried Pad Thai rice noodles
- 225g (8 oz) chicken breast, thinly sliced
  - 1 tsp Thai thin soy sauce (for marinade)
  - 1 tsp cornstarch (for marinade)
  - 1 tsp water (for marinade)
- 20g (¼ cup) dried shrimp, minced or processed into a coarse powder
- 3 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 large shallots, thinly sliced (or ½ red onion)
- 2 tbsp preserved Thai salted radish, rinsed (or zha cai / Chinese mustard stem, julienned; optional)
- 3 large eggs, beaten, at room temperature
- 200g (2 cups) mung bean sprouts, washed and drained
- 50g (1 cup) Chinese garlic chives, cut into 2.5cm (1 inch) pieces
- 20g (2 tbsp) roasted peanuts, finely chopped
- 6 tbsp vegetable oil, divided

## Instructions

### Make the Sauce

1. Break up the tamarind pulp into the boiling water. Let soak a few minutes, then mash and press through a fine-mesh strainer; discard solids. (If using tamarind concentrate, skip straight to the next step.)
2. Stir in sugar, fish sauce, black soy sauce, sweet soy sauce (if using), and white pepper. Set aside.

### Prep

3. Soak noodles in hot water for 20 minutes, then drain. If the strands are very long, cut into 25–30cm (10–12 inch) lengths for easier stir-frying.
4. Toss chicken with soy sauce, cornstarch, and water. Set aside to marinate.
5. Prep all remaining ingredients before turning on the wok — dried shrimp, garlic, shallots, salted radish, eggs, sprouts, chives, and peanuts. Pad Thai moves fast once the heat is on.

### Stir-Fry

6. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wok over high heat until just smoking. Add chicken and sear until golden and just cooked through. Remove and set aside.
7. Add 3 tbsp oil to the wok over medium heat. Add shrimp powder and fry until fragrant and crisp, about 2 minutes. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds. Add shallots and preserved radish, stir-fry 30 seconds more.
8. Turn heat to high. Add noodles and sauce. Stir-fry, lifting and separating the noodles until the sauce is absorbed and everything is well combined.
9. Push noodles to one side of the wok. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil to the cleared space and pour in the beaten eggs. Fold gently to scramble — don't break them up too finely. When about 70% set, stir-fry the egg into the noodles.
10. Add bean sprouts and chives and stir-fry until the chives just wilt. Return chicken to the wok and toss to combine.
11. Plate and top with crushed peanuts. Serve with lime wedges.
