A delicious 1-loaf rye bread that is simple enough to kneed by hand.

http://www.thecrepesofwrath.com/2017/01/30/beginners-rye-bread/

## Equipment

-   One 4-1/2 x 8-1/2 inch loaf pan.

## Ingredients

-   7 grams (2¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
-   1 cup + 2 tablespoons warm water (about 110-115° F)
-   1½ tablespoons molasses- divided into ½ tablespoon and 1 tablespoon
-   30 grams (1oz) (2 tablespoons) unsalted butter, melted
-   1 tablespoon caraway seeds, plus more for topping
-   10 grams salt
-   255 grams (9oz) (480ml / 2 cups) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
-   160 grams (6oz) (1¼ cup) rye flour
-   1 egg white, for brushing

## Instructions

### Yeast

1.  In a large bowl, sprinkle **yeast** over 60ml (¼ cup) of **warm water**.
2.  Add in **½ tablespoon molasses** and whisk until the yeast
    dissolves.
3.  Let the mixture stand for about **5 minutes**, until foamy.

After 5 minutes...

4.  Add in and mix to combine:
    -   Remaining water
    -   Molasses
    -   Melted butter
    -   Caraway seeds
    -   Salt

### Combine with flours and first rise

1.  Gradually add in **rye** and **all-purpose** flours, one cup at a
    time, until the dough comes away from the sides of the bowl and
    forms a sticky ball (add more warm water if too dry).
2.  Knead the dough on a floured surface for about **5 minutes**.
3.  Shape into a ball and transfer to a large buttered/oiled bowl.
4.  Cover tightly with plastic wrap, and set in a warm place to rise for
    about **1 hour**, or until **doubled in size**.

### Prepare loaf pan and second rise

1.  Butter a 4-1/2 x 8-1/2 inch loaf pan and set aside.
2.  When the dough has risen, **punch it down** and on a lightly floured
    surface
3.  Roll the dough into a 7 x 7-inch square, then fold it into thirds,
    and pinch the ends to seal.
4.  Place the dough, seam-side-down, in the loaf pan and brush with egg
    white.
5.  Sprinkle the top of the dough with caraway seeds.
6.  Spray a piece of plastic wrap with cooking spray and place it on top
    of the dough.
7.  Let stand for **45 minutes to 1 hour**, until the dough touches the
    plastic wrap.

### Bake

1.  Preheat oven to **450° F**.
2.  Once heated to 450° F, reduce temperature to **400° F**.
3.  Remove plastic wrap from loaf pan, place in oven, and bake for
    **45-55 minutes**, rotating the pan halfway through baking, until
    the top is golden brown.
4.  Turn out bread onto a cooling rack.
5.  Let cool a least **30 minutes** before slicing.
