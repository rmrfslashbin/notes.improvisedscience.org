A simple yet flavorful preparation of Brussels sprouts, enhanced with the savory umami of white miso paste and a touch of sweetness from honey or maple syrup. The hot cast iron skillet helps achieve a beautiful caramelization on the cut surfaces of the sprouts.

## Yield
- Serves 3-4 as a side dish

## Special Equipment
- Large cast iron skillet

## Preheat Oven
1. Temperature: 400°F (205°C)
2. Position rack in center of oven
3. Place cast iron skillet in oven to preheat

## Ingredients
- Brussels sprouts, halved (approximately 450g (16oz) or 1 pound)
- White miso paste (30g or 2 tablespoons)
- Neutral oil like grapeseed or avocado (15ml or 1 tablespoon)
- Rice vinegar (5ml or 1 teaspoon)
- Honey or maple syrup (15ml or 1 tablespoon)
- Garlic, minced (2 cloves)

## Preparation
1. Wash and trim **Brussels sprouts**, cutting them in half lengthwise
2. In a large bowl, combine **white miso paste**, **neutral oil**, **rice vinegar**, **honey** (or **maple syrup**), and **minced garlic**
3. Toss halved **Brussels sprouts** in the miso mixture until evenly coated

## Bake/Cook
1. Carefully remove the hot cast iron skillet from the oven
2. Place the coated Brussels sprouts cut-side down on the hot skillet
3. Return skillet to oven and roast at 400°F (205°C) for **20-25 minutes** until crispy on the outside and tender inside
4. The sugars in the miso and honey will help them caramelize beautifully
