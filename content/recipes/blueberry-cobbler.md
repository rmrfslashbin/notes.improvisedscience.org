> This cobbler, which comes from the kitchens of Chez Panisse, prizes
> the berries above all, using only 1/3 cup of sugar. The dough rounds
> for the top are placed so they don't cover all the berries, and the
> juice from the berries bubbles up around the dough.

## Equipment

-   1 ½-quart gratin or baking dish

## Preheat Oven

Preheat the oven to 375 degrees.

## Ingredients

### The berries

-   765g (27oz, 4 ½ cups / \~2.5 pints) fresh blueberries
-   60g (2oz) sugar
-   12g (1 ½ tablespoon) all-purpose flour
-   Zest of one lemon
-   1 tablespoon fresh squeezed lemon juice

### The Dough

-   192g (7oz, 1 ½ cups) all-purpose flour
-   ½ teaspoon kosher salt
-   18g (1oz, 1 ½ tablespoons) sugar
-   2 ¼ teaspoons baking powder
-   85g (3oz, 6 tablespoons) cold unsalted butter, cut into 1/2-inch
    pieces
-   180ml (¾ cup) buttermilk

## Preparation

### Step 1 (Berries)

1.  To prepare the **berries**, place in a bowl.
2.  Toss with the **sugar,**flour**,**lemon zest'''.
3.  Pour **lemon juice** over berries and lightly toss.

### Step 2 (Dough)

To make the dough whisk together, in a bowl:

-   Flour, spelt, or half flour/spelt
-   Salt
-   Sugar
-   Baking soda

### Step 3 (Cut in butter)

1.  Cut in the **butter** until mixture resembles coarse meal.
2.  Add the buttermilk and mix lightly, just until the dry ingredients
    are moistened.
3.  Add more flour or splet to thicken of too thin

### Step 4 (Build cobbler)

1.  Put the blueberries in a 1 ½-quart gratin or baking dish.
2.  Make patties out of the dough, 2 to 2 ½ inches in diameter and
    ½-inch thick.
3.  Arrange the patties over the top of the berries.

### Step 5 (Bake)

1.  Bake until the topping is brown and the juices bubble thickly around
    it, about **35 to 40 minutes**.

## Serve

1.  Let cool slightly.
2.  Serve warm, with: cream, ice cream, or whipped cream, if desired.
