This recipe is perfect for a cold winter night. The warm spices of south
France and Spain combine to flavor this slow cooking dish. If you don't
have any spices from a Provençal market, my [Moroccan Seasoning]({{< ref "/recipes/moroccan-seasoning" >}}) recipe is a very solid
stand-in.

This recipe contains no definite quantities; use it as a basic map and
improvise (as I did) to create your own version of this delicious dish.

## Special Equipment

-   Large, heavy bottom stew pot

## Ingredients

-   Chicken thighs; bone in, skin removed
-   Dried apricots
-   White mushrooms
-   Onion
-   Peppers
-   Potatoes
-   [Moroccan Seasoning]({{< ref "/recipes/moroccan-seasoning" >}})
-   Salt to taste
-   Chicken (or other) stock
-   Cous-Cous
-   Brown sugar

## Preparation

1.  Chop vegetables, add to pot.
2.  Add chicken thighs.
3.  Add apricots.
4.  Fill with enough stock and/or water to cover.
5.  Add [Moroccan Seasoning]({{< ref "/recipes/moroccan-seasoning" >}}).
6.  Simmer for two or so hours, till chicken is cooked and vegetables
    are soft.

### Taste

Additions of salt, seasoning, and sugar should be done in moderation and
slowly. Add, stir, taste, let simmer, taste again... add more of
whatever as needed. Repeat.

1.  Add salt and/or additional [Moroccan Seasoning]({{< ref "/recipes/moroccan-seasoning" >}}) as needed.
2.  Add 1 to 2 tablespoons brown sugar (to taste) to temper the
    bitterness.

### Cous-Cous

In a saucepan, prepare cous-cous.

1.  Typically, bring 480ml (2 cups) water and/or stock to a boil.
2.  Remove from heat and stir in 120ml (½ cup) or so of dry cous-cous.
3.  Cover saucepan and let sit 5 minutes.
4.  Remove cover and "fluff" cous-cous with a fork.

## Presentation

For each serving:

1.  Add some cous-cous to a bowl.
2.  Top with chicken and vegetables.
3.  Add a desired amount of broth.
