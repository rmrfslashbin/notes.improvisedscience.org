## Equipment
- Large skillet, I prefer a 12" to 15" cast iron
- Large Dutch oven

## Ingredients
- Olive oil
- Onions (two to three, I use one red and one yellow medium-sized onions)
- 1 shallot
- 7 to 8 Garlic cloves
- 3 bell peppers, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 3 zucchini, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 yellow squash, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 eggplants, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 kilo tomatos, cut into bite-sized pieces
- Several springs of Thyme
- 1 to 2 sprigs of Rosemary


## Prep
1. Cut all vegetables into bite-sized pieces, keeping each vegetable in its own bowl.
2. Peel the garlic cloves and slice if desired.

## Method
### Onions, Shallots and Garlic
1. Heat the skillet over medium heat.
2. Add olive oil to the skillet.
3. Add the onions and shallots to the skillet and cook until browned and slightly caramelized.
4. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant.
5. Remove the onions and shallots from the skillet and put in the Dutch oven.

### Bell Peppers
1. Add olive oil to the skillet.
2. Cook the bell peppers until soft and slightly browned.
3. Remove the bell peppers from the skillet and put in the Dutch oven.

### Zuichini and Yellow Squash
1. Add olive oil to the skillet.
2. Cook the zuichini and yellow squash until soft and slightly browned.
3. Remove the zuichini and yellow squash from the skillet and put in the Dutch oven.

### Eggplant
1. Add olive oil to the skillet.
2. Cook the eggplant until soft and slightly browned.
3. Remove the eggplant from the skillet and put in the Dutch oven.

### Tomatos and Herbs
1. Add the tomatos to the Dutch oven.
2. Stir the contents of the Dutch oven to combine.
3. Add the thyme and rosemary to the Dutch oven.

### Simmer
1. Simmer the contents of the Dutch oven for at least 40 minutes.

## Notes
- I like to serve this with a crusty bread and a nice red wine.
- Ratatouille can be served hot or cold.
- The dish can also be served with gnocchi or pasta.
- The flavors of the dish can be enhanced with a little soy sauce to add depth and umami.
- Paprika can be added to the dish to add a little heat.
