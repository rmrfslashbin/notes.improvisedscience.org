Super easy pan-seared Salmon.

## Source

https://cooking.nytimes.com/recipes/1014578-four-spice-salmon

## Ingredients

-   4 168g (6-ounce) skinned salmon fillets
-   Salt and black pepper
-   1 tablespoon coriander seeds or ground coriander
-   ¼ teaspoon whole or ground cloves
-   1 ½ teaspoons cumin seed or ground cumin
-   1 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg
-   2 tablespoons peanut oil, grape seed or other neutral oil, or
    clarified butter

## Preparation

1.  Season fillets on both sides with salt and pepper.
2.  Toast spices then grind them to a coarse powder in a coffee or spice
    grinder.
3.  Press some of the mixture onto the top of each fillet.
4.  Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat for 2 or 3
    minutes.
5.  Add the oil or butter and, when it shimmers, place the fillets,
    coated side down, in the pan.
6.  Cook about 2 to 3 minutes, or until the spice mixture forms a nicely
    browned crust.
7.  Turn the fillets and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes, or until the
    salmon just slightly resists when pierced with a thin-bladed knife.
